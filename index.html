Fun springtime activities in NJ to explore – Six Flags Great Adventure & Safari

If fun is what you are looking for, Six Flags Great Adventure & Safari brings it in spades!

ix Flags Great Adventure is an amusement park located in Jackson, New Jersey. In actuality, the Park opened up in 1974. However, it now has eleven themed areas. In 2012, the park merged with the 350-acre Wild Safari animal park, making it the second-largest theme park in the world! Above all, it has world-class coasters, three kids’ areas and events like Fright Fest and Holiday in the Park. There’s also a massive water play area that has twenty-five thrill slides, a massive wave pool, just to name a few. In the summer of 2020, the park is going to open the world’s tallest, fastest, and longest single rail coaster called the Jersey Devil Coaster. Which will definitely be worth checking out!

Liberty State Park

Liberty State Park among other things offers beautiful views. That’s why it’s perfect for sight seeing enthusiasts!

Visit Us

The Liberty State Park is just as the name implies, a park that is located in Upper New York Bay in Jersey City. It opened two years after the Six Flags Great Adventure amusement park (1976). However, that doesn’t mean it isn’t big. It spans 1,212 acres of land! And it has multiple points of interest you can explore. So, let’s check a few of them out.

  • Freedom Way and Liberty Walkway. Freedom Way goes through the center of the park. It is a railing that separates the public and private areas of the park. It has a lot of bike paths, walkways, and fields. Whilst the Liberty Walkway is for all intents and purposes, a promenade that spawns from Central Railroad of New Jersey to the Statue of Liberty overlook. Making it a perfect choice to check out!

  • Liberty Science Center. The LSC is an interactive science museum and learning center. It has one of the largest planetaria in the world! Making it a must-see for any celestial navigation connoisseur!

  • Monuments and memorials. There are plenty of monuments and memorials to explore. From the Liberation statue that was sculpted in memorial to the victims of the Holocaust, to the Empty Sky, a state memorial to the 9/11 attacks.

So, you might need to schedule more than just a day or two… If you really want to explore all the amenities the Liberty State Park has to offer.
